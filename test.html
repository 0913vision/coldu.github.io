Definition

Let X∞:=X∪{∞} (as a set), T∞:=T∪{X∞}∪{W⊂X∞ | ∞∈W,X∞∖W is closed and compact in X}

Then (X∞,T∞) is called the 1-point compactification of (X,T).

 

Theorem

(a) T∞ is indeed a topology on X∞.

(b) (X∞,T∞) is compact.

(c) (X,T) is a subspace of (X∞,T∞), i.e., (T∞)X=T (restriction)

(d) X∞=(X∞,T) is Hausdorff ⇔ X is Hausdorff & locally compact.

(e) X is a dense subset of X∞ ⇔ X is not compact.
